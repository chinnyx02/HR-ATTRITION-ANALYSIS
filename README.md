HR Employee Attrition Analysis Dataset
Overview

This dataset contains Human Resources (HR) employee information used to analyze employee attrition within an organization. Attrition refers to employees leaving the company due to resignation, retirement, termination, or other reasons.

The dataset helps analysts identify factors affecting employee retention, job satisfaction, work-life balance, salary structure, and workforce performance.

Dataset Purpose

The main goal of this dataset is to:

Analyze employee turnover
Identify causes of attrition
Understand employee behavior
Improve workforce retention strategies
Support HR decision-making using data


Key Insights
Employees working overtime showed higher attrition rates.
Sales and Research & Development departments experienced the highest turnover.
Employees with low job satisfaction were more likely to resign.
Younger employees and employees with fewer years at the company had higher attrition tendencies.
Salary differences exist between junior and senior employees.

Conclusion

The HR Employee Attrition dataset provides valuable insights into workforce behavior and employee retention. By analyzing employee demographics, salaries, overtime, satisfaction, and experience levels, organizations can make data-driven HR decisions to improve employee engagement and reduce attrition
